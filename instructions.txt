You will also need to add the following snippet to your JBoss config under <datasources>
for the persistence to work correctly.

Creating the persistence schema is required, and of course change the user-name and password fields.

    <datasource jndi-name="java:/Persistence" pool-name="persistence" enabled="true" use-ccm="false">
        <connection-url>jdbc:mysql://localhost/persistence</connection-url>
        <driver-class>com.mysql.jdbc.Driver</driver-class>
        <driver>mysql</driver>
        <security>
            <user-name>root</user-name>
            <password>root</password>
        </security>
        <validation>
            <validate-on-match>false</validate-on-match>
            <background-validation>false</background-validation>
        </validation>
        <statement>
            <share-prepared-statements>false</share-prepared-statements>
        </statement>
    </datasource>
    <drivers>
        <driver name="mysql" module="com.mysql">
            <xa-datasource-class>com.mysql.jdbc.Driver</xa-datasource-class>
        </driver>
    </drivers>
